Exercise 51.1

Let \( H(s,x) : I \times X \rightarrow Y \) be a homotopy between \( h \) and \(h'\); and \( K (t,y) : I \times Y \rightarrow Y\) be the homotopy between \(k \) and \( k'\). Then, a homotopy between \( k \; o\; h\) and \( k' \; o \; h'\) can be defined by

\begin{align} H :& I \times k(Y) \rightarrow Z, \\ H :& (s, k(y)) \mapsto H (t, K((t,y)). \end{align}

It can be checked that this is indeed a homotopy by substituting \( t = 0\) and \( t = 1.\)

Exercise 51.2

a) As \( I \) is a convex subset of \( \Re\), a straight line homotopy always exists between any two functions \( f,g : X \rightarrow I\). Hence \( [X,I]\) has a single element.

b) Y is path-connected,
\( \Rightarrow \forall \; y_0, y_1 \in Y \quad \exists \; \text{path} \; \gamma \quad \text{such that} \; \gamma(0) = y_0, \gamma(1) = y_1. \) We can use these paths to construct a homotopy between \( f(i) \; and \; g(i) \) for example, \begin{align} H : &I \times I \rightarrow Y, \\ H : &(i,t) \mapsto \gamma_i(t), \end{align} where, \( \gamma_i(t) \; \text{is a path such that} \; \gamma_i(0) = f(0) \; and \; \gamma_i(t) = g(t) \).
This homotopy is continuous in t but it does not ensure continuity in i.
Thus, we use the fact that \( [I,I] \) has a single element, which implies that the identity map \( i : I \rightarrow I\) and a constant map \( c_{x_0} : I \rightarrow 0 \) are homotopic to each other. Suppose \( f : I \rightarrow Y\), then \( f \; o \; i (: I \rightarrow Y) \simeq f \; o \;\; c_{x_0} (: I \rightarrow f(x_0) ) \). Therefore, any map \( g : I \rightarrow Y\) is null homotopic (to \(g(x_0)\)). Now, as Y is path connected, we can find a homotopy (using the path) between \( f(x_0) \; \text{and} \;g(x_0)\). To summarise, we have that for any \( f,g : I \rightarrow Y\), \begin{align} f(x) &\simeq f(x_0), \\ f(x_0) &\simeq g(x_0), \\ g(x_0) &\simeq g(x) \\ \Rightarrow f(x) &\simeq g(x). \end{align} Therefore \( [I,Y ]\) has a single element for any Y.

Exercise 51.3

a) As both I and \( \Re\) are convex, there exists a straight line homotopy between any constant map and the identity map of the set. Thus, they are contractible. In fact, any convex subset of \( \Re^n\) is contractible.

b) Let \( H (x,t) : X \times I \rightarrow X \) be the homotopy between \( c_{x_0} \) and \( id : X \rightarrow X\). This homotopy provides a path between \( x_0 \) and all other points of X. Let \begin{align} H (x,0) = x_0, \\ H(x,1) = x. \end{align} A path \( \gamma \) between \( x_0 \) and \( x_1 \in X\)can be defined as \begin{align} \gamma(t) = H(x_1,t). \end{align}

c) As Y is contractibe, any map \( f : X \rightarrow Y \) is homotopic to a constant map, suppose \( c_{y_0}\) (using similar arguments as in 2 (b)). Thus, for any maps \( f,g : X \rightarrow Y\), \begin{align} f &\simeq c_{y_0}, \\ g &\simeq c_{y_0} \\ \Rightarrow f &\simeq g. \end{align}

d) Let, \begin{align} id : X \rightarrow X, \\ c_{x_0} : X \rightarrow x_0, \\ f : X \rightarrow Y, \end{align} \( \Rightarrow (f \; o \; id), (f \; o \; c_{x_0}) : X \rightarrow Y \). Now, as X is contractibe, \( f \; o \; id \; \simeq f \; o \; c_{x_0} (= f(x_0)). \) Similarly, for any other map \( g : X \rightarrow Y; i.e., g(x) \simeq g(x_0)\). As Y is path connectd, \( g(x_0) \simeq f(x_0) \implies f(x) \simeq g(x).\)
